# Analysis strategy

## General aim

Evaluate soil enzyme activity as a diagnostic component of soil health in irrigated Palmer mango orchards in the Brazilian semiarid region.

## Main indicators

The initial biological indicators to be evaluated are:

- beta-glucosidase activity;
- arylsulfatase activity;
- geometric mean of enzyme activities (GMea).

Derived or normalized indicators may also be evaluated, including enzyme activity normalized by clay content, soil organic matter, or other relevant soil properties.

## Main response variable

Relative yield per plant will be used as the primary productivity response, because it reduces confounding from planting density and orchard architecture.

Relative yield per area may be retained as a secondary sensitivity response.

## Diagnostic targets

The analysis will distinguish three related but different diagnostic goals:

1. Minimum biological level: a lower threshold below which low relative yield is more likely.
2. Critical adequacy threshold: a cut point that separates low- and high-performing orchards.
3. Biological reference range: the range of enzyme activity associated with high relative yield.

## Candidate methods

The following approaches will be compared:

- Cate-Nelson analysis for critical adequacy thresholds;
- shallow recursive partitioning for minimum or decision-rule thresholds;
- ROC/Youden-type thresholds when binary productivity classes are used;
- distribution-based reference ranges among high-yielding orchards;
- sensitivity analyses using different productivity-class definitions.

## Interpretation

Cate-Nelson will be treated as a candidate method for agronomic critical levels.

Recursive partitioning will be treated as an exploratory method for identifying simple diagnostic rules and possible lower thresholds.

Reference ranges will be interpreted as dataset-specific biological ranges associated with high relative yield, not as universal thresholds.

## Data policy

The original dataset is private and must not be committed to the repository.

Synthetic data will be used for public workflow demonstration.
