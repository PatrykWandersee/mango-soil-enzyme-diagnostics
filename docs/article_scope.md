# Article scope

## Working title

Soil enzyme diagnostics for irrigated Palmer mango orchards in the Brazilian semiarid region

## Central objective

Develop and evaluate diagnostic approaches for interpreting soil enzyme activity in irrigated Palmer mango orchards, with emphasis on beta-glucosidase, arylsulfatase, and the geometric mean of enzyme activities (GMea).

## Main analytical focus

- beta-glucosidase activity;
- arylsulfatase activity;
- geometric mean of enzyme activities (GMea);
- relationships with clay, soil organic matter, salinity/sodicity indicators, and relative yield;
- comparison between raw and normalized enzyme indicators;
- biological interpretation of enzyme activity in perennial irrigated orchards;
- potential diagnostic thresholds or reference ranges.

## Tentative separation from the SQI article

The SQI article focuses on multidimensional Soil Quality Index construction and internal validation.

This article focuses on biological indicators and enzyme-based diagnostic interpretation. The calibration of enzyme-specific thresholds, biological reference values, or diagnostic classes belongs to this manuscript rather than to the SQI manuscript.

## Data policy

The original dataset is private and must not be committed to the repository.

Public examples will use synthetic data generated for workflow demonstration only.
